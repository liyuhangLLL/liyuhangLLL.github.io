Simulation for digital holograms on tilted planes

https://opg.optica.org/oe/fulltext.cfm?uri=oe-13-24-9935&id=86288

$$
\hat{O}(d;u,v) = O(u,v)exp[i2\pi d (\lambda ^{-2}-u^2-v^2)]
$$

$$
u=\xi/\lambda d, v=\eta/\lambda d
$$

$O(u,v) = F{o(x,y)}$ is the spectrum of the object at $z=0$. We then rotate the wave vector coordinates $k=2\pi[u,v,w],w=(\lambda ^{-2}-u^2-v^2)^{1/2}$.

Consider 3D rotation matrix (clockwise rotation):

we can get rotated wave vector components $k=2\pi[\hat{u},\hat{v},\hat{w}],\hat{u}=u\cos\theta -w\sin \theta, \hat{v} =v,\hat{w}=u\sin\theta+w\cos\theta$.

$$
u = \hat{u}\cos\theta + \hat{w}\sin\theta
$$

Therefore, $\hat{O}(d;\hat{u},\hat{v})=\hat{O}(d;\hat{u}\cos\theta+\hat w\sin\theta,\hat{v})$

Before application of the inverse Fourier transform, the grids have to be mapped into a uniformly spaced frequency grid using interpolation methods.

Finally, $o(\hat{x},\hat{y}) = F^{-1}\hat{O}(d;\hat{u}\cos\theta+\hat w\sin\theta,\hat{v})$
